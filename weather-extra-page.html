Tropical Climate of Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka experiences a tropical climate throughout the year. The country is located close to the equator, which means it has consistent temperatures and high humidity. The average temperature ranges from 25°C to 30°C (77°F to 86°F) in the lowlands and coastal areas, while the central highlands are cooler, with temperatures averaging between 15°C to 18°C (59°F to 64°F). The island receives two monsoons, the southwest monsoon (Yala) from May to September and the northeast monsoon (Maha) from November to February, which brings heavy rainfall to different regions.

Impact of Climate Change on Sri Lanka

Like many other countries, Sri Lanka is vulnerable to the impacts of climate change. Rising sea levels and increased frequency of extreme weather events, such as floods, landslides, and droughts, are some of the major concerns. These changes have implications for agriculture, water resources, coastal communities, and biodiversity. The government and various organizations are working to address these challenges through adaptation and mitigation strategies.

Seasonal Tourist Destinations in Sri Lanka

Sri Lanka's climate plays a significant role in shaping its tourism industry. Different regions of the country are popular during specific seasons. For example, during the southwest monsoon (Yala), the west and south coasts experience rainy and windy conditions, making the east coast, including Trincomalee and Arugam Bay, more attractive to tourists seeking sunny beaches and water sports. Conversely, during the northeast monsoon (Maha), the west and south coasts become popular as the weather is drier and more suitable for beach tourism.
